New York City, New York, USA

Geographical Location: North America

New York is considered an influential cultural center of the world. Representatives of all nations, continents, and cultures live in this unusual city.

New York is composed of five boroughs: Brooklyn, the Bronx, Manhattan, Queens and Staten Island - is home to 8.4 million people who speak more than 200 languages, hail from every corner of the globe, and, together, are the heart and soul of the most dynamic city in the world.

London, England, UK

Geographical Location: Europe

London is the capital city of the United Kingdom. It is the U.K.'s largest metropolis and its economic, transportation, and cultural centre. London is also among the oldest of the world's great cities, with its history spanning nearly two millennia.

As one of the most iconic cities in the world, London carries a rich history that is matched by its continuous growth and evolution. A powerhouse of culture, politics, and finance, it stands as a city where the past and present exist harmoniously side by side. From the ancient towers that narrate tales of a regal past to modern architectural marvels that reach for the skies, London offers an unrivaled blend of history and modernity.

Paris, France

Geographical Location: Europe

Paris (nicknamed the "City of light") is the capital city of France, and the largest city in France. The area is 105 square kilometres (41 square miles), and around 2.15 million people live there.

Paris is one of the most distinctive cities in the world. It is a city has its own character, especially because it was accompanied by beauty, art, culture and revolution.

Tokyo, Japan

Geographical Location: Asia

Tokyo is located at the head of Tokyo Bay on the Pacific coast of central Honshu. It is the focus of the vast metropolitan area often called Greater Tokyo, the largest urban and industrial agglomeration in Japan.

Tokyo is the administrative, cultural, financial, commercial, and educational centre of Japan and the focus of an extensive urban complex that includes Kawasaki and Yokohama. Attractions include the Imperial Palace, encircled by stone-walled moats and broad gardens, and numerous temples and shrines.

Rome, Italy

Geographical Location: Europe

Rome is the capital city of Italy. It is also the capital and largest city in the region of Lazio, and the geographical region of Latium. It is on the Tiber River and has 2.8 million people.

Rome is often referred to as the City of Seven Hills due to its geographic location, and also as the "Eternal City". Rome is generally considered to be the "cradle of Western civilization and Christian culture", and the centre of the Catholic Church.

Los Angeles, California, USA

Geographical Location: North America

Los Angeles is the city with the second biggest population in the United States after New York. It is also the biggest city of California. It is a car-centric city requiring much parking, and one of the most spread out cities in the world.

Los Angeles, also known as the City of Angels, is known for its amusement parks, Hollywood culture, beautiful neighborhoods and eclectic artistic spirit. Bustling Los Angeles is home to Hollywood stars, high-fashion shopping, enchanting beach spots and culture.
